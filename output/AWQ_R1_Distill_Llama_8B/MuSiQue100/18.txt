The region north of Israel where the Battle of Qurah and Umm al Maradim occurred was established as the Kingdom of Moab. This area is located in present-day Jordan.

The Kingdom of Moab was established in the early Iron Age, around the 12th century BCE, by the Israelites under King Mesha. The Battle of Qurah and Umm al Maradim took place during the reign of King Mesha, when he successfully defended his territory against the Mesopotamian forces.

So, the region was established as the Kingdom of Moab, and the battle occurred during the early Iron Age, around the 12th century BCE, in present-day Jordan.
